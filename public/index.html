Mental Health Awareness

Your mind matters. Take care of it.

What is Mental Health?

Mental health refers to our emotional, psychological, and social wellbeing. It affects how we think, feel, and act in everyday life.

Mental health is important for everyone, including both men and women. Taking care of your mental wellbeing helps improve quality of life.

Myth vs Fact

  • Myth: Mental health problems are rare.
  • Fact: Many people experience mental health challenges.
  • Myth: Asking for help is weak.
  • Fact: Asking for help is a strength.

Common Mental Health Issues

Stress

Feeling overwhelmed by pressure.

  • Tension
  • Irritability
  • Difficulty relaxing

Anxiety

Ongoing worry or fear.

  • Racing thoughts
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Restlessness

Depression

Persistent sadness or low mood.

  • Low energy
  • Loss of interest
  • Sleep changes

Panic Attacks

Sudden intense fear.

  • Shortness of breath
  • Dizziness
  • Chest discomfort

Signs You Should Not Ignore

  • Constant worry
  • Fast heartbeat
  • Sleeplessness
  • Mood changes
  • Loss of focus

Mental health problems are real and treatable. Getting support can make a positive difference.

Self-Care Tips

  • Deep breathing exercises
  • Regular sleep routine
  • Healthy daily habits
  • Talking to someone you trust
  • Limiting social media use

How to Help Someone Else

  • Listen without judgement
  • Be patient and supportive
  • Avoid harmful phrases
  • Encourage professional help

When to Seek Professional Help

  • Symptoms lasting more than two weeks
  • Frequent panic attacks
  • Feeling hopeless
